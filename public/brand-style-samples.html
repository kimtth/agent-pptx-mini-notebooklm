Theme Priority And Contrast Rules

The app follows the internal rule set from styles.md: theme tokens decide the actual colors, every text color must maintain contrast, and each style should keep its signature layout technique even when the palette changes.

Theme wins Backgrounds, text, accents, and borders are mapped to workspace theme tokens rather than hardcoded colors.
Contrast first Light-on-light and dark-on-dark combinations should be corrected before export.
Style = technique Each style keeps its composition rules, signature forms, and pacing even when the theme changes.
Use samples fast Scan the cards for tone, then read the cue line for the defining move that makes the style feel authentic.

Layout Pattern Templates

Reusable micro-patterns for card interiors. These are meant to work inside the selected style rather than replace it.

Use Icon Card for big signals and Header Icon Card for compact structured categories.

2-4 cardsFeature, pillar, benefit

Icon Card

Give each card one prominent icon embedded inside the card body, then stack title and copy with a clear hierarchy.

Cue: integrated icon block, bold first read, body copy clearly separated below.

3-6 cardsCapability, step, role

Header Icon Card

Reserve a thin header band for small icons and a short heading, then keep the body copy in a separate lower block.

Cue: slim header strip, small repeated icons, text mass kept below the divider.

Template Motifs From External References

Original reference sketches derived from recurring layout ideas observed across Canva, Slidesgo, and Visme template libraries.

These are reusable presentation motifs, not new selector entries. They help extend the sample board beyond the 30 named styles.

Motif 01
Editorial Split Hero
Left anchor, right stage
Pitch, proposal, keynoteHero image or quote

Editorial Split Hero

A narrow anchor column holds identity or navigation while the main stage carries one hero visual and one dominant message.

Cue: asymmetric split, generous whitespace, one decisive headline block.

Observed across Canva minimalist pitch decks and Slidesgo minimalist business templates.

Motif 02
Diagonal Block Narrative
Angle-driven flow
Launch, strategy, campaignGraphic emphasis

Diagonal Block Narrative

Large diagonal geometry turns a simple business slide into a directional composition with built-in momentum.

Cue: skewed shapes, color wedges, content tucked into calm pockets between the diagonals.

Observed across Canva diagonal block templates and geometric corporate decks.

Motif 03
KPI Dashboard Strip
Metric-first density
Reports, reviews, opsData visualization

KPI Dashboard Strip

A compact header strip introduces the story, while the lower zone breaks into metric modules for fast comparison.

Cue: summary ribbon up top, modular widgets below, restrained but high-contrast data framing.

Observed across Visme report templates, visual charts decks, and Slidesgo annual-report layouts.

Motif 04
Geometric Proposal Grid
Structured block logic
Business plan, proposalSection mapping

Geometric Proposal Grid

The slide becomes a set of disciplined tiles, making scope, timeline, resources, and next steps easy to segment.

Cue: square-ish modules, even gutters, selective accent cells that guide the scan path.

Observed across Slidesgo Geometric Pattern decks and Canva square-shape presentation templates.

Motif 05
Accent Monochrome Focus
One color, one punch
Executive summaryStatement slide

Accent Monochrome Focus

Most of the slide stays neutral so one saturated block or one keyword can carry the emphasis without noise.

Cue: black-and-cream base, single accent surface, typography used as the main visual lever.

Observed across Visme black-and-white splash-of-color guidance and Canva monochrome pitch decks.

Motif 06
Process Timeline Ribbon
Step sequence with pace
Roadmap, onboarding, workplanSequential story

Process Timeline Ribbon

A horizontal sequence line establishes order, while the lower panel holds the explanation and keeps the slide readable.

Cue: connected step markers, concise labels, one lower explanation band instead of scattered captions.

Observed across Canva infographic timeline templates and Slidesgo workplan or meeting layouts.

Motif 07
Organic Editorial Canvas
Soft brand storytelling
Creative brief, lifestyle brandSoft premium tone

Organic Editorial Canvas

A calm neutral field, one softened image block, and one biomorphic accent create a more tactile brand narrative layout.

Cue: warm beige base, rounded image window, one organic mass instead of hard geometric framing.

Observed across Canva organic creative project templates and soft editorial presentation decks.
